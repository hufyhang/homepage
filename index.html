Hi! I am currently a PhD candidate at School of Computer Science, The University of Manchester. I am extremely passionate about front-end development. During my spare time, doing Web development side projects is one of the favorite stress-relief activities of mine.

Research

My PhD research mainly focuses on building platform to support service composition from the perspective of End-User Software Engineering. So far, I have 5 papers published in top-class conferences (ICWS2013, ICWS2014, and ICWS2015) and international journal (IJSC).

As another part of my research outcomes, the following Web services and platforms have been release to public:

  • HyperMash: A Web-based service composition platform prototype.
  • Hyperportal: A Web portal for all the composite services published in HyperMash.
  • Service Spotlight: A primitive services search engine.

Front-End Development

Since the beginning of my PhD research, I started to learn front-end development techniques by myself by doing different side projects no matter how big or small they are.

HTML, CSS, and JavaScript are the three major parts of my coding life. I happened to use jQuery quite frequently during the early stage of my main research project. After that, inspired by several other frameworks (e.g. YUI and AngularJS), I began to work on my own JavaScript front-end framework, called ChopJS, to (a) better learn JavaScript and front-end development concepts and techniques, and (b) transfer my research outcome HyperMash to a better designed and more scalable platform.

I am also experienced in PHP and Node.JS.
